How it stays honest
Phenology is monitored on a fixed roster of tagged plants along transects, so this is a timing signal (when each phenophase happens), not a measure of how common a species is — labelled everywhere. Onset is interval-censored to the midpoint between the last "no" and first "yes" visit, honest about the twice-weekly resolution, with left-censored rows flagged. Growing extent is leaf-active days (distinct leaf-weeks × 7), not a green-up-to-leaf-off span, so a drought-deciduous desert plant that flushes twice a year isn't credited a 300-day season. The Phenology Clock pools years by design; year-to-year shifts live in the onset trend, which refuses to fit fewer than 5 years and says "no detectable shift" when the interval spans zero. Sample-size gates everywhere (≥5 obs per phenophase-week, ≥3 plants per species-year) and a colour-vision-deficiency-safe palette. The app loads pre-bundled snapshots — no live-API waits.
