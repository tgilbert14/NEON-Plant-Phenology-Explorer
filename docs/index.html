What you can do

Read the phenology clock

The typical year as overlapping seasonal petals — where the greens peak is leaf-out, the pink is bloom, the amber is autumn. Switch species to compare how each times its year.

The Onset Lab

Every tagged plant on one board — when it wakes (green-up onset) × how many days a year it carries leaves. Tap a dot to pin its card, then open its full career.

Pick a site on the map

The landing is a national map of every NEON site — dot size is plants tagged, color is median green-up (green = early spring, amber = late). Tap a dot to dive in.

Follow one plant

Each tagged individual becomes a downloadable card — green-up / first-flower / leaf timing, its phenophase calendar across years, and low-noise quality checks (PNG + CSV).

Across sites — the gradient

Median green-up vs latitude across the network: spring marching later as you move north — the spatial echo of Hopkins' bioclimatic law — plus the same species across its range.

Take the data with you

A one-click analysis-ready export — tidy CSVs of observations, onsets, per-plant summaries, the clock and the trend, plus a codebook decoding every column and method.

Statistics — timing, not abundance

Phenology is monitored on a fixed roster of tagged plants along transects, so it is a timing signal (when each phenophase happens), not a measure of how common a species is. Every view leads with transparent methods:

  • Onset is interval-censored to the midpoint between the last "no" and first "yes" visit — honest about the twice-weekly resolution — and rows where the first visit was already "yes" are flagged left-censored.
  • Growing extent is "days carrying leaves" (distinct leaf-weeks × 7), not a green-up-to-leaf-off span, so a drought-deciduous desert plant that flushes twice a year isn't credited a 300-day season.
  • The clock pools years by design (the typical season); year-to-year shifts live in the onset trend, which refuses to fit fewer than 5 years and says "no detectable shift" when the confidence interval spans zero.
  • Sample-size gates everywhere: a phenophase-week needs ≥ 5 observations, a species-year needs ≥ 3 plants, and the colour palette is colour-vision-deficiency safe.
