# Plant phenology observations (green-up onset) — Expert Review by Iris (NEON DP1.10055.001)
_Devoted product-expert review — June 2026._

> Iris here. I walked this app the way I walk a green-up number: from the tagged plant on the loop, through the interval-censored `onset()`, out to the cross-site gradient — and I'll say plainly that this is the cleanest sibling in the suite and the one place the whole cascade earns a defensible rung. The timing-not-abundance framing is everywhere, the onset is honestly interval-censored to the midpoint (Diez et al. 2012 done right — I checked it against HARV: 55.4% of onsets land on a `.5`, mean first-yes→onset gap 2.47 d, exactly the half-interval of twice-weekly visits), `leaf_off` is correctly *not* first-coloration, `leaf_active` is the honest growing extent, the QC ordering check is suppressed for the drought-deciduous forms that legitimately flush twice, and every verdict refuses small-n or prints a CI that admits when it spans zero. So the machinery is gold and I am telling you not to touch it. My quarrel is narrow and it is the same one I always have: **in warm deserts, green-up onset is not a weak metric — it is the WRONG phenophase, scored for a fifth of the roster — and the app still leads with `median_greenup` in the hero, the map, and the national gradient without a coverage badge that says so.** Surface the coverage share, make `leaf_active`/first-leaf the desert metric, and lead the cross-site read with the within-species slope. That is the difference between handing Cass a green-up signal and handing Cass a temperate construct in a desert's clothing.

## Method fidelity (is the NEON protocol represented correctly?)

Faithful — this is the part I have the fewest fights with.

- **The unit and the design are right.** The about panel (`ui.R:460`) and codebook (`R/codebook.R:17-18, :91`) state the unit as a *tagged plant individual* with repeated measures, `plotID` as the spatial block, visited "up to twice a week through the growing season." That is the PHE protocol (NEON.DOC.014040): the ~800 m phenology loop with up to 100 tagged individuals plus the phenocam plot. The "fixed roster, not a census" caveat is in the Overview card (`ui.R:73`), the insight list (`server.R:145`), and the export README (`server.R:378-380`). I cannot fault the design representation.
- **Status-based scoring is represented correctly.** The bundler keeps `phenophaseStatus ∈ {yes, no, uncertain}`, drops `missed`, and *excludes `uncertain` from every denominator* (`bundle_phe_data.R:41`; codebook `:23`, `:92`). That is the single most important QC rule of this product and it is honored everywhere. The explicit "no" is what makes onset estimable, and the app uses both yes and no in `onset()` (`phe_helpers.R:54`).
- **Phenophases are growth-form-specific and USA-NPN-aligned** (Denny et al. 2014). The `GREENUP` set (`phe_helpers.R:26`) correctly spans the four growth-form green-up onsets — *Breaking leaf buds* (broadleaf), *Initial growth* (forb/graminoid), *Emerging needles* / *Breaking needle buds* (conifer). The decode block (`codebook.R:70-84`) is textbook-accurate, including the conifer analog of flowering (*Open pollen cones*) and the count-vs-percent intensity distinction.
- **The intensity trap is handled before it can bite.** `intensity` is carried as an *ordinal, phenophase-specific bin*, and the codebook (`:24`, `:95`) forbids averaging it across phenophases (leaf/flower = percent-of-canopy bins; fruit/cone = geometric count bins — incommensurable). The app never numericizes intensity. Good — that is the trap that sinks naive phenology analyses, and it is closed.
- **The two source tables are the right two** — `phe_statusintensity` (the signal) joined to `phe_perindividual` (the roster), `bundle_phe_data.R:34-50`. No third table is invented; identity is denormalized onto obs and `nativeStatusCode` is re-joined only at export to save RAM. Correct.

One labelling precision to tighten (method-level, not a bug): the Phenology Clock denominator `n` (`phe_helpers.R:135-137`) counts yes-or-no **observation records** per phenophase-week pooled across years, not distinct individuals. A plant visited twice in a calendar week contributes 2. The captions and codebook say "% of plants" (`ui.R:91, :100`; `codebook.R:60`). It is an honest *visit-weighted* status index, but it is not literally "% of plants" — re-label it "% of observations 'yes'" or de-duplicate to one record per individual×week before the rate. Small, but I do not let labelling slide on a denominator.

## Analysis & metrics — defensible? (with the literature)

Yes — and the onset metric specifically is journal-defensible, which I do not say lightly.

- **Interval-censored onset is the correct estimand and is implemented correctly.** `onset()` (`phe_helpers.R:58-61`) takes the midpoint between the last preceding "no" and the first "yes", and flags `left_censored` when there is no preceding "no". This is exactly Diez et al. 2012 (*Ecology Letters*) — phenology onset is interval-censored by the discrete visit grid, raw first-yes is biased late by up to one visit interval, and the midpoint is the defensible point estimate. The verified HARV numbers (DATA-TAKEAWAYS, line 27-30) confirm it behaves: 2.47 d mean gap = the half-interval of ~3-day visits, only 0.3% left-censored. A phenology reviewer would accept this.
- **The #1 trap of this product — visit-interval bias masquerading as biology — is acknowledged but not fully guarded.** The app censors correctly, which removes the *constant* part of the bias. What it does not yet do is *protect cross-site/cross-year onset comparisons against unequal visit cadence*. Two sites with different effective visit frequency will show an "onset difference" that is partly censoring geometry, not plant biology (this is the phenology analog of detection probability in wildlife). The cross-site gradient (`server.R:409-426`) and the same-species panel (`:442-455`) compare `median_greenup` across sites with no cadence check. **Fix:** carry a per-site median-visit-interval (or median gap behind each onset) and either footnote it on the gradient or down-weight sites with coarse cadence. This is the one analysis discipline I would still add.
- **`leaf_active` is the right growing-extent metric and the literature backs the choice.** Counting distinct leaf-weeks × 7 (`phe_helpers.R:100-102`) instead of a green-up→leaf-off span is correct for multi-flush drought-deciduous plants — the span fabricates a ~300-day season while the real carried-leaf extent is ~110. The codebook even ships the reconciliation warning that `leaf_off − greenup ≠ leaf_active` (`codebook.R:38`). One caveat to add: `leaf_active` is **also visit-cadence-sensitive** — it has a hard 7-day floor (one scored leaf-week → 7 days) and undercounts at sites with sparse visits (a leaf-present week never visited is never credited). It is the honest metric, but it is not cadence-immune; say so in the codebook beside the `leaf_off` warning.
- **The onset-trend slope is honest small-n statistics.** `trendInsight` (`server.R:207-225`) refuses to fit fewer than 5 annual points, reports days/**year** (never extrapolated per-decade), prints the 95% CI, and prints "no statistically detectable shift … spans zero" when the CI straddles zero. The per-year medians are never pooled (`onset_trend`, `phe_helpers.R:147-153`), and each plant is collapsed to its earliest green-up first so a plant scoring two green-up phenophases isn't pseudo-replicated. This is exactly the small-n honesty the suite needs — and it matters because each species-year point rests on a *median n = 5 individuals* (71% at n = 3-6), a false-negative regime where per-site verdicts are dishonest. The "a signal, not a verdict … partly reflects which species were monitored" caption (`:223`) is the right hedge. Keep it.
- **The cross-site gradient is real but reported confound-first instead of confound-controlled.** The network slope `median_greenup ~ lat` = +2.5 d/°N (R² 0.41) pools different species mixes at every site. The cleaner read — *Acer rubrum* alone, +4.4 d/°N, R² 0.78 across 10 sites — is the confound-controlled gradient (holding species constant nearly doubles both slope and R²), and the app *already computes it* in the "same species across its range" panel (`server.R:442-455`). Yet the headline insight banner (`gradientInsight`, `:427-435`) leads with the pooled network slope and reports **no CI** in the banner. This is the spatial echo of the suite's one robust temporal link (warmer spring → earlier green-up, pooled ~32 sites, p ≈ 0.01) — two independent geometries of the same temperature mechanism, which is genuinely strong evidence. **Fix:** lead with the within-species slope, print its CI, and demote the network slope to "coarse across-network echo." The app has the better read sitting in the secondary panel; promote it.

## What the field would add (collection / analysis / presentation / use)

- **Collection / calibration:** tie the ground onset to the tower **PhenoCam Gcc** curve (Richardson et al.; the phenocam plot is *designed* to overlap the camera field of view for exactly this). The 25%/50% greenness-amplitude thresholds for "spring onset" are the land-surface-phenology counterpart to the ground onset, and they should agree (and disagree informatively — ground = individual species, cam = aggregate canopy). A "ground onset vs canopy Gcc" panel would be the single highest-value addition and is squarely within the protocol's intent. Even a caption noting the Gcc complement would help.
- **Analysis:** think in **growing-degree-days / chilling**, not raw calendar correlation. Onset is mechanistically a heat-sum threshold crossing — that is *why* temperature → green-up is the robust link. A GDD-anomaly framing (accumulate °C-days above a base; onset ≈ threshold day) would convert the descriptive slope into a mechanistic one and is the standard the thermal-time literature expects. The app is calendar-correlation today; that is defensible, but GDD is the upgrade.
- **Analysis (censoring, restated):** a hierarchical/survival model of onset (Diez et al. 2012) that propagates the interval-censoring and the per-plant random effect would be the journal-grade version of the median-of-medians the app ships now. Not required for an explorer, but it is what a reviewer would ask for in a paper.
- **Presentation:** the biome-conditional metric switch (below) is the one presentation change the field would insist on. Beyond that, a per-site **visit-cadence badge** ("median 3.4 d between visits") would let users judge onset comparability themselves.
- **Use:** frame green-up shifts in terms of **phenological mismatch** (pollinators, migrating birds) — the app's About panel already gestures at this (`server.R:464-465`), and it is the highest-value "so what" for an agency reader. Lead the use-case with mismatch, not abstract climate fingerprinting.

## Product-specific honesty & QC traps

The traps I own are mostly handled. Scorecard of each, with the one that still bites:

- **Interval-censoring** — handled (`onset()`, midpoint + `left_censored`). The residual: no cross-site/cross-year **cadence-comparability** guard. Add it.
- **Green-up is a temperate construct in warm deserts** — *only partly handled.* This is my headline criticism. At HARV/SCBI 91-92% of tagged plants resolve a finite green-up onset; at **SRER only 19%** (40/215), JORN 54%, MOAB 61%, ONAQ 63% (DATA-TAKEAWAYS line 35-40). Desert drought-deciduous/cactus/evergreen plants rarely record a green-up *phenophase* — they are scored straight into "Leaves"/"Young leaves" — so a desert `median_greenup` is a small, non-random subsample, not a noisy forest number. The app surfaces NONE of this in-app: the hero "median green-up" stat (`server.R:118`), the national map color (`server.R:69-70, 87`), the per-plot map (`server.R:395`), and the cross-site gradient all present desert green-up with forest-grade confidence. **Fix (two parts):** (1) a **coverage badge** — when the green-up share < ~0.5, render "green-up scored for 19% of plants here — read leaf-active" beside the hero stat and on the map legend; (2) a **metric switch**, not just a caption — in warm-desert biomes the default plant signal should be `leaf_active` / first-"Leaves"-week, with green-up demoted. `leaf_active` survives where green-up collapses (SRER 67%, ONAQ 99% coverage; site medians 35-266 d span the whole network). This is the data-level expression of the suite's biome-conditional-priors truth, and it is the one change I will not let slide.
- **`leaf_off` ≠ senescence** — handled correctly. `leaf_off` is the last "Leaves"=yes day, *explicitly not* first "Colored leaves" (`phe_helpers.R:73-76, 87, 97`), because NEON faithfully logs `Colored leaves: yes` for a handful of early-stressed leaves (a red oak in June). The card caption (`server.R:333-334`) and codebook (`:37`) both warn it is "last leaf-week, not measured senescence." Guard this; it is right.
- **The clock pools years; the trend does not** — handled and firewalled. The clock is captioned "pooled across years" (`server.R:177`, `ui.R:93`); between-year shifts live only in `onset_trend` (per-year, never pooled). Conflating them would invent a trend from a climatology — the app keeps them separate. Right.
- **Timing, never abundance/productivity** — handled, labelled in ~6 places. Phenology complements veg-structure basal area (the slow state floor); it does not replace richness or productivity. The app never claims otherwise.
- **Structural-missing ≠ failure** — partly handled. `flower` is NA for whole non-flowering growth forms (monitored, structurally absent) vs NA for "monitored, never yes." The codebook explains it (`:36`) but the per-site export has no `flower_n_years` companion to let a downstream analyst tell the two apart at the row level. Low-priority add.
- **Conifer vs broadleaf green-up are not directly comparable** (needle-flush timing differs). The cross-site *P. strobus* series is non-monotonic with latitude — a *fair read*, not an error. The app maps *Emerging needles* into `GREENUP` correctly; it should add a one-line note that conifer and broadleaf green-up aren't directly comparable on the same axis.
- **Denominator label** — the clock "% of plants" is actually "% of observations" (visit-weighted). Re-label or de-duplicate.

## Place in the suite / cascade

This app produces the **climate → plants** rung's most defensible link in the entire suite, and it is the rung Cass should lead with. The temporal signal (warmer spring → earlier green-up, pooled across ~32 sites, p ≈ 0.01) is corroborated **here in space**: +2.5 d/°N across the network and +4.4 d/°N within red maple alone (R² 0.78) — the same temperature mechanism in two independent geometries. What I hand Cass, with the discipline attached:

1. **Lead with green-up, and lead with the within-species gradient.** It is the cleanest evidence the suite owns. Network slope is the coarse echo; red-maple slope is the controlled read.
2. **Carry the metric switch the biome demands.** Green-up onset in temperate biomes; `leaf_active` / first-leaf-week in warm deserts, because the green-up *phenophase* is scored for only ~1/5 of desert plants. This operationalizes biome-conditional-priors at the data level, not just the prior level — and it is why the cascade must not feed a desert `median_greenup` downstream as if it had forest-grade coverage.
3. **Treat phenology as timing, not productivity** — corroborate against veg-structure basal area, never substitute for richness.
4. **Inherit the honesty machinery unchanged** — interval-censoring, n-gates, CI-or-silence verdicts, pooled-not-per-site discipline. They are all correct here; propagate them downstream.

The styling/chrome is full flagship parity (splash national picker, demo-on-startup with HARV present, hero count-up, pin-card Onset Lab, downloadable QC card, shipped 72-row codebook, CI-honest banners, dark mode). I'm not the styling authority, but nothing in the chrome distorts the science, which is my bar.
